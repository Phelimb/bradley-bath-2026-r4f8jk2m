Hot Tub

The hot tub is eco-friendly, using no chemicals or electricity. It is heated by a wood burner attached to the tub — firewood and kindling are provided.

While heating, stir the water with the paddle to mix the hot water with the cold. Allow 90+ minutes for the water to reach temperature.

The water can be used for up to 3 days, after which it will need replacing.

Cinema

To turn on the cinema screen and projector, press the green ON button on the top left of the large remote control, pointing it at the projector.

To turn it off, press the red OFF button on the top right. Volume is already set at an appropriate cinema level.

Feel free to log into the Batheaston House streaming accounts — but please don't purchase anything using the house's account. Disney+ can be a bit slow sometimes.

Heating & Fireplaces

Heating and hot water will always be on. The radiators have wooden disc valves — turn anti-clockwise to turn on, clockwise to turn off.

Operational fireplaces: Kitchen · Drawing Room · Dining Room

Firewood and kindling are provided. Please do not leave open fires unattended.

All other fireplaces are sealed — please don't attempt to use them.

The fire alarm panel is in the 'Housekeeping Cupboard' in the basement hall. Carbon monoxide detectors are in each room with a fireplace.

Things To Do in Bath

Attractions
The Roman Baths
Amazingly well-preserved. Holograms, actors, artefacts from the Sulis Minerva temple — the most popular attraction in Bath.
romanbaths.co.uk
Bath Abbey
Twelve and a half centuries of history. Extraordinary ornate carvings including Jacob's Ladder. Tower tours available.
bathabbey.org
Royal Crescent
Iconic curved row of 30 Grade I listed Georgian houses, designed by John Wood the Younger (1767–1774). UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Pulteney Bridge
One of only four bridges in the world with shops across its full span on both sides. Designed by Robert Adam, 1769.
The Canal Walk
Serene walk past narrowboats, aqueduct and country pubs. A short drive from the house.
Bath Skyline Walk
6 miles, 3–4 hours. Best views of Bath through meadows and ancient woodland above the city. Starts near the centre.
Botanical Gardens
In Royal Victoria Park, formed 1887. Six acres of quiet parkland with a wide selection of flora.
Spas
Thermae Bath Spa
Natural thermal waters at the heart of the city. Rooftop pool with unforgettable views. Two-hour sessions, treatments and massages available.
thermaebathspa.com
Gainsborough Bath Spa
Luxurious spa tapping Bath's original thermal waters. Circuit of natural thermal pools, saunas, steam rooms and ice alcove.
The Bath House
Within the Royal Crescent Hotel gardens. Japanese bath house theme with relaxation pool and treatments. Advance bookings essential.
